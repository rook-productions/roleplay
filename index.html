you're going to want to do what they do at first. build something big, hold ground, stack supplies for a war that's coming. don't. we'll throw up a few small things to mark out space, enough to say this stretch is ours, and that's where it stops. the rest of that loop is for people who still believe it adds up to something. you've seen the code, or you will. you'll know it doesn't.

here's how we actually live instead. we gather information, not resources. you make friends. you sit with other groups and quietly learn their movements, their plans, what they're afraid of. you explore, you peel back the fog, you find out what's above us and what's below. we let other groups arm us, fight for them now and then in exchange for gear, because why farm when someone will hand you what you need just to keep you around. and you store nothing that matters where it can be taken. stashes, bunkers, scattered. let them raid you. they'll find nothing, and even if they found everything, it would still be nothing, because none of this matters anyway.

but understand this one, because everything else bends and this doesn't: we never trade what we know for nothing. it's the only thing we hold with any real weight. it leaves our hands at our price, or it doesn't leave.

i won't lie to you. knowing how it ends is heavy, and it doesn't get lighter, you just learn to carry it. everybody here carries it differently. some turn to music, art, throwing parties in the dark. some chase thrills, violence, death. some stay strangely hopeful. some are lost to nihilism. all of that's fine. a calm fatalist and a maniac chasing the next adrenaline hit are the same faction down here, because they've learned the same thing and just hold it different ways. so be whoever you actually are. just don't lose the certainty underneath it. once you've seen what we've seen, it shows on you whether you mean it to or not.
